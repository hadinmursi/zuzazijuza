WWE Wrestler Candice Lerae Is Happily Married To Her Husband Johnny Gargano

WWE champion Candice Lerae is married to Johnny Gargano. The two tied the knot on 16 September 2016 at Disneyland.

The two initially crossed paths when both of them were competing in independent wrestling. They went on their first date to a Subway when performing an independent wrestling event.

As time passed, their relationship grew, and the two performers eventually became inseparable.

Johnny, Lerae’s then-boyfriend-now-husband, formally became a WWE Superstar in 2016. They officially got engaged in the same year.

Candice accompanied her hubby in the WWE in May 2017, and the two of them went on to achieve a great deal in NXT.

The WWE champion has often highlighted the advantages of marrying a man who effectively performs the same duties and holds the same status.

They enjoy taking trips together, and frequently experience a Disney ride or go on a moving date.

On the occasion of their 5th wedding anniversary, Johnny penned a sweet note for Candice sharing their throwback wedding images on Instagram. 

He conveyed his gratitude to his wife for improving his life by being around him. He also expressed his joy in becoming a father.

Candice Lerae Was Raised By Her Parents In Los Angeles

Candice Lerae was born to her parents in the Los Angeles suburb of Riverside, California, on 29 September 1985.

Her mom and dad named her Candice LeRae Dawson at his birth. The former NXT Women’s Tag Team champion holds a zodiac symbol, Libra.

Her parents are proud of her accomplishments in the WWE career. She has won the FWE Women’s Championship and the Ironman Heavyweight Championship under her belt.

Many publications reported her as a native of Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. Lerae claims that this misunderstanding arises from a choice made by an independent promoter early in her fighting career.

Dawson was advertised as a Canadian to create heat since the promoter in question didn’t think she would make a good heel.

Candice’s family was happy to learn about her WWE debut when she fight as one of the contenders in a battle royal for Asuka’s NXT Women’s Championship on 3 May 2017.

Then, she participated in her debut women’s Royal Rumble match at the Royal Rumble on 27 January 2019, starting at position 17 and going for 9:35 minutes until being defeated by Ruby Riott.

She fought in the second WrestleMania Women’s Battle Royal on 7 April at the WrestleMania 35 pre-show, where the fighter was defeated by Asuka.

On 3 January 2016, she made her professional wrestling debut for the Japanese organization DDT Pro-Wrestling (DDT), defeating Golden Storm Riders, along with Joey Ryan.

Likewise, she wrestled for Tokyo Joshi Pro Wrestling (TJP), the all-female sister organization of DDT. On 4 January 2016, she won her debut match against Yuka Sakazaki.

Candice Lerae Grew Up Watching Pro Wrestling Along With Her Siblings

Ironman Heavymetalweight title holder Candice Lerae had an amazing childhood with her siblings. She has three loving brothers in the family.

When Candice was a senior in high school, her brother, Warren, was adopted by her parents, as she shared via Twitter in 2020.

Warren is one of the most incredible individuals. Candice is incredibly proud of him and fortunate to have him as a brother.

As a child, she used to watch professional wrestling with her brothers. Eventually, she developed a passion for the profession.

Her older brother had a big impact on her career. He took Candice to her first independent wrestling event, and he always paid for it.

According to Candice, her brother introduced her to TNA, and PWG, as well as purchased numerous VHS tapes of professional wrestling from throughout the world.

On the occasion of his birthday, the WWE star shared that through her brother’s enthusiasm they were introduced to many wonderful people. He continues to make an effort to give her wrestling advice.

Nevertheless, she has many male cousins and was frequently the lone female when they would play sports together.

Lerae first played the flute and piccolo in a marching band before attending an unidentified institution and majoring in culinary arts. She also spent a few years working as a baker at Universal Studios Hollywood.
