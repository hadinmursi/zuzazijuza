Bernadette Peters Biography

Bernadette Peters is an American actress, singer, and children’s book author who was born on February 28, 1948, in Ozone Park, New York, the United States as Bernadette Lazzara. Over a career spanning five decades, Bernadette has starred in musical theatre, television, and film, performed in solo concerts, and released recordings.

Bernadette is a critically acclaimed Broadway performer, having received seven nominations for Tony Awards, winning two (plus an honorary award), as well as nine nominations for Drama Desk Awards, winning three. Four of the Broadway cast albums on which Bernadette has starred have won Grammy Awards.

Regarded by many as the foremost interpreter of the works of Stephen Sondheim, she is particularly noted for her roles on the Broadway stage, including in the musicals Mack and Mabel (1974), Sunday in the Park with George (1984), Song as well as Dance (1985), Into the Woods (1987), The Goodbye Girl (1993), Annie Get Your Gun (1999), Gypsy (2003), A Little Night Music (2010), Follies (2011), and Hello, Dolly! (2018).

Bernadette first performed on the stage as a child and then a teenaged actress in the 1960s, as well as in film and television in the 1970s. Bernadette was praised for this early work and for appearances on The Muppet Show, The Carol Burnett Show, and in other television work, and for her roles in films including Silent Movie, The Jerk, Pennies from Heaven, and Annie.

In the 1980s, Bernadette returned to the theatre, where she became one of the best-known Broadway stars over the next three decades. Bernadette also has recorded 6 solo albums and several singles, as well as many cast albums, and performs regularly in her own solo concert act.

Bernadette continues to act on stage, in films, and on television in such series as Smash and Mozart in the Jungle. Bernadette has been nominated for three Emmy Awards and three Golden Globe Awards, winning once.

Bernadette Peters Family

Bernadette was born into a Sicilian-American family in Ozone Park in the New York City borough of Queens, the youngest of 3 children. Bernadette’s mother, Marguerite (née Maltese), started her in show business by putting her on the television show Juvenile Jury at the age of 3 and a half, and Bernadette’s father, Peter Lazzara, drove a bread delivery truck. Bernadette’s siblings are casting director Donna DeSeta and Joseph Lazzara.
